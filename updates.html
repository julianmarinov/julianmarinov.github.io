003

I've been away from the matter for few months. When I tried to get some work done, I realized that there is nothing in my head. I understand something people frequently say about learning software development - It's way better to spend 20 minutes a day doing some small tasks rather than working few consecutive days 5-6 hours. When time passes, nothing stays in your mind. However, in just few days I almost recovered all that seemed lost. And it didn't work by just watching a course from the beginning. It worked when I started solving simple problems with the most basic JavaScript operators. I got faster and faster, then I jumped to more difficult tasks ad was actually easy. So my conclusion is: If you want to learn - solve problems. If you want to refresh your knowledge - solve problems. If you want to acheave anything big - just solve problems. One after another.
